The counties in focus have since experienced exponential population increase in the last decade, based 2019 census report. The estimate stands at 5.3 million people with high poverty levels reflected by more than 80% of the total population living on an income of less than one dollar per day and thus exposing the population to life-threatening social economic activities.Further to this, the region bares the largest share of the HIV/AIDs disease burden with the four counties of Homabay, Siaya, Kisumu, and Migori being the top four in HIV/AIDS prevalence ranking and percentage figures three times higher than the national prevalence of 4.9% except for Busia with slightly elevated prevalence 5.1% compared to the national average. 
In addition to inherent socio-economic challenges, the County’s population remains deeply rooted in traditional cultural practices, adding to strong ties with political systems whether beneficial to the development agenda or not. 
These circumstantial sceneries have resulted in the counties lagging in development agenda, with economic activities related to agriculture, environmental conservation, technology application, infrastructure, and education sectors below the par compared to other regions considered as developed.
Because of these issues, ​LAVICO–EDP has come up with solutions and projects that will enable communities in the  counties to empower themselves all around.       

Mission

LAVICO-EDP endeavors to promote a sustainable socio-economically empowered society through creative, innovative, and sustainable practices of agriculture, environmental conservations, health interventions, educational growth programs, promotion of good governance, and sustainable economic inclusive development.   

Vission

LAVICO–EDP envisages a healthy and wealthy empowered Population through Participating in the development, growth, and economic sustainable programs.

Objectives

LAVICO-EDP's overall objective is to promote and expand access to quality education, healthcare services, good governance, conservation of environments, and Improve agricultural production, water, and hygiene to the poor and vulnerable society. The organization's specific objectives to be ​accomplished within its powers and coverage include:

• To promote access to affordable quality education, instituting bursary fund support to the needy students in secondary and vocational institutions and also promote CBC compliance in the public Institutions within the vulnerable areas through expanding and equipping their learning structures, especially in the Early Year Education (EYE) framework.

• To influence the health agenda, increase accountable resources for universal access to equal Reproductive, Maternal, New-born, Child, and Adolescent Health and Nutrition (RMNCAHN) by the vulnerable groups through rights-based approach and integration within the existing government interventions
including response to emerging trends/issues such as COVID-19 and HIV/AIDS.

• To promote agribusiness management practices, adapt and build the resilience of sustainable agricultural practices and food security systems to climatic
change at multiple levels to the vulnerable society.

• To promote proper conservation of environmental practices along Lake Victoria through training on green technology, control of pollution, and human
activities among the vulnerable society.
• To provide access to safe water and proper hygienic practices among the poor society

• To advocate and promote good governance for both private and public entities for sound economic management, proper utilization of public resources, and policy development through training to improve the economic and livelihood of the poor society.

• To raise, secure and utilize funds for the attainment of any or all objects of the Organization, and to do such other things as are incidental or conducive to the attainment of these objects

Strategic Program Approach 

Lake Victoria Community Empowerment and Economic Inclusive Growth Development Program focuses to meet its fiscal budget through diverse sources which include local and international grants and donations. The program targets both adults and children living with HIV/AIDS, Youths, and other vulnerable categories in the population particularly the neglected elderly people, orphans, and their caregivers. LAVICO-EDP targets the community in need of provisions of basic services, equipment, and basic material goods combined with capacity building for enhanced life skills through training, advocacy, and information advice for governance including substantial livelihood for individual households. The activities and service interventions implemented by LAVICO-EDP are community sensitive through the incorporation of development approaches that consider inclusivity, participation of all segments of the population, and technological solutions for efficiency and sustainability.
